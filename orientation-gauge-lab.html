The Orientation-Gauge Experiment CONJ → measured

Cut a pure global state into two sides L | R with no interaction across the cut. Prop 7.2 says the relative time-orientation of the two sides is gauge: nobody living inside side R — with rods and clocks built from side-R physics — can tell whether L's modular time runs forward or backward (P7.2a), nor whether L is the faithful mirror partner or a scrambled purification with the same ρR (P7.2b). Both were tested adversarially on a two-sided fermion thermofield double with verdict rules fixed before the code was written. Everything below is measured data (exact correlation-matrix dynamics, no Trotter error).

The honest construction. The R-observer's rod is an impurity bound-state width (static binding length), its tick the measured beat of a small cavity carved into R. The battery: clock ratios, two-time correlators in rods/ticks, a KMS thermometer, a quench entropy arrow, purity/entanglement-spectrum floors — six dimensionless items, side-R data only. Calibration: every god's-eye discriminator must fire at g=0 (it does, d ≈ 0.4–1.4) and every internal item must fire once the cut opens at g=0.1 (it does, d ≈ 0.02–1.1). Gates G0–G9 include a dense Fock-space cross-check of the whole machinery at 10−15.

The crossover: how fast orientation becomes physical

The gauge property is exact only at g = 0. Opening the cut, the internal battery detects the complement's micro-state at first order in g but its time-orientation only at second order — the O(g) term rides on the number-conserving cross-correlation ⟨c†LcR⟩, which vanishes identically for the TFD:

D(O+ vs O−) ∝ g² · D(mirror vs scrambled) ∝ g  (measured slopes 1.98 and 1.02)

Measured D_internal vs g at N=256, β=1. The g=0 points sit at float noise (10⁻¹⁵) — plotted in the left gutter. The vertical marker tracks the slider above.

Verdict (pre-registered rules). P7.2a HOLDS: max D_internal(O+ vs O−, g=0) = 2.0×10⁻¹⁵ across N ∈ {64…512}, β ∈ {0.5, 1, 4} — six decades under tolerance — with all positive controls live. P7.2b HOLDS at 1.4×10⁻¹⁴ — but this one is a theorem (every R-observable is a functional of ρR); the run demonstrates the machinery realizes it, and calibrates the battery that makes the null meaningful. Both ΘL implementations (flipped generator; conjugation sandwich) give identical verdicts. Full write-up: docs/results-writeup-orientation.md.

What this does not show. No claim about our cosmological vacuum — this is the idealized eternal-TFD construction. Gaussian states carry no pointer basis, Born rule, or branch structure: this tests orientation gauge, not branching. And one amendment was logged before the run: the bare cavity coherence vanishes by a parity selection rule, so the clock is "wound" by an R-local phase kick, identical in every variant.
