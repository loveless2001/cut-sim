The Ruler-Cancellation Experiment CONJ → measured

The framework's mechanism for hiding the lumpy cone: an observer's rods and clocks are made of the same couplings as the signals it measures, so the directional factor should cancel in every dimensionless speed ratio. That is falsifiable — so it was tested, adversarially, with verdict rules fixed before running. Everything on this page is measured data from that experiment (exact free-fermion dynamics, 2D lattice with Jx ≠ Jy).

The honest construction. The observer's rod in each direction is the width of an impurity bound state — a static binding length, never site-counting, never "the distance light travels" (that would make the answer 1 by definition). Its clock is a cavity beat period — one shared tick. Its signals: (a) the quench front — its fastest signal; (b) a wavepacket whose wavelength it sets with its own rods. The tuning knob is the binding strength: weak binding → rod size ξ → ∞ = approach to the continuum (an RG fixed point, "criticality"). A detection battery was calibrated first on god's-eye data, where it must flag the known anisotropy — and did.

What the experiment established

1 · Naive claim falsified

An observer timing its fastest signal against its own rods measures a direction-dependent speed that plateaus at a non-cancelled value near the fixed point. "No dimensionless internal experiment detects anisotropy" is false. The front is a UV probe pinned to the band edge; it never renormalizes.

2 · A continuum scaling law

Rods scale as √Jd (measured rod ratios match √r to four decimals at large ξ); ballistic fronts scale as Jd. So the observer sees the anisotropy square-rooted: Aendo(front) → √Agod — e.g. 1.4195 measured vs √2.0075 = 1.4169. The rods absorb half the anisotropy in the continuum regime, never all of it.

3 · Corrected claim survives — IR only

Long-wavelength wavepacket observables co-disperse with the rods and become isotropic at the fixed point (Aendo → 1.0002 at ξ ≈ 39). Spreading central values move the same way but are tail-limited at the largest rods. Emergent Lorentz invariance is real but infrared: exact only at the fixed point, and only for observables that flow to it.

4 · The open question

Free theories can't close the loophole: the UV front simply doesn't renormalize. Whether an interacting fixed point makes the front itself co-disperse — removing the UV leak — is the natural next test, needing tensor-network rather than Gaussian dynamics. Honest status of the framework's Lorentz claim: plausibly rescued at interacting fixed points, unproven, undecidable in a free theory.
