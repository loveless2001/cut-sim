The framework's first co-product: cut a system in two, and the subsystem's entanglement entropy SA = −Tr(ρA log ρA) starts to change — the framework reads its growth as A forgetting its complement, and a monotone reparametrization of it as the subsystem's clock, τA.

Below is a real quantum system evolving in your browser — a chain of free fermions prepared in a Néel state (particles on alternating sites) and released. The dynamics are exact (analytic eigenmodes, correlation-matrix evolution — the same method as the research code, no numerical integration). The chain is cut at a movable point; the curve is the entanglement entropy of the left part.

What to notice

1 · The whole has no clock; the part does. The global state stays pure — SU = 0 at every instant, nothing "happens" to the whole. Only the cut subsystem has a growing quantity. This is Proposition "global purity, local mixedness": the clock is a co-product of the partition, not a property of the universe.

2 · Move the cut, change the clock. Drag the cut position: a different bipartition gives a different SA(t) — a different rate of forgetting, a different τA. Time here is cut-relative. And no rule inside the theory says which cut is the right one (Turtle 2).

3 · The entropy comes back down. GAP Let it run — especially at N = 16. After the fast rise, SA(t) fluctuates and partially revives: unitary dynamics is time-symmetric and recurrent, so nothing makes SA monotone. On these small chains you can watch the "forgetting" partially un-forget. The arrow — what makes forgetting stick, what turns SA into a usable time-order — is not derived. It must be imposed as a low-entropy boundary condition (the past hypothesis, Turtle 1). What you are watching is the precise reason the framework needs its first turtle.

Physics: open-boundary tight-binding chain, quench from the Néel state; SA from the spectrum of the reduced correlation matrix. Identical method to src/lattice-model-exact-fft-evolution.py, ported to JS.
