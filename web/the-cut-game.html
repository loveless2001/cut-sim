The Cut — the game

One universe, two views. God's-eye sees the whole — the full state, the substrate, the facts no inhabitant can reach. Cut makes you a subsystem: you see only what a subsystem can register. The toggle above is the entire game. Everything below runs on the same exact free-fermion physics as the research code (overview), and Level 5 uses the measured data from the pre-registered experiment — nothing there is faked.

How to win. You don't, mostly. This framework's integrity comes from its limits — two irreducible "turtles" and one falsified claim. The gaps are playable here: some levels are unwinnable by design, and the badges you collect are limits found, not victories. If you finish thinking "that was honest," the game worked.

The quest is unwinnable, and that is the lesson. The framework searched for a partition selector and reports none: no eigen-decomposition, no symmetry, no variational rule inside the theory picks a preferred cut (its Claim 10.1 GAP — "Turtle 2"). Every cut is as valid as any other; "which cut" is an input, not an output. The only winning move:

WHAT THIS SHOWS

  • Concrete selector candidates fail for concrete reasons on a real system: instability in time, degeneracy, or smuggled god's-eye facts.
  • The absence of a canonical cut is a load-bearing gap the framework states about itself, made playable.

WHAT THIS DOES NOT SHOW

  • A proof that no selector can exist — four failed selectors on one lattice is an illustration, not a theorem GAP.
  • That cut-relativity makes physics arbitrary: given a cut, everything downstream is fixed and exact.

Level 6 — Two views of one paradox

Analogy only, stated up front: this level illustrates a shape, it derives nothing. In particular it does not derive the island rule or solve either paradox CONJ. It shows that two famous puzzles have the same grammatical form: a cut-relative quantity mistaken for an absolute one.

The problem of time

The universe's global state satisfies its constraint exactly — like your Level-1 block, it just sits there. S_U = 0. Where did time go?

 

The information paradox

Radiation from an evaporating black hole looks thermal — entropy rising past every bound. Where did the information go?

 

WHAT THIS SHOWS

  • Both puzzles dissolve their sting the same way: stop demanding a cut-independent fact, ask the cut-relative question instead.
  • In the black-hole case, the entropy that follows the Page curve is computed by optimizing over cuts (the island prescription) — discovered independently of this framework, and credited below.

WHAT THIS DOES NOT SHOW

  • Any derivation. The island rule comes from gravitational path integrals; Page–Wootters time from constraint quantization. This level borrows their shape.
  • That the analogy is exact — treating it as such would be precisely the overclaiming this game exists to avoid.

Where the seams are

You did not solve physics. Neither does the framework — it dissolves some questions and marks its own limits. What a finished player holds:

The two turtles stand. The arrow of time is imported (Level 4) and the cut is unchosen (Level 3). The one falsifiable mechanism tested so far came back partly falsified, partly supported (Level 5). That is the honest state of the art here — boundaries understood as clearly as claims.
