The Cut

Co-products of a partition — an explorable companion to the working spec (v0.3) and its one numerical experiment.

The thesis in one paragraph. Take a closed quantum universe: one Hilbert space, one pure state, one Hamiltonian, evolving unitarily. Nothing in there flows, leaks, or forgets. Now draw a cut — split the universe into a subsystem A and its complement B. Suddenly three quantities exist that didn't before: time (the growth of A's entanglement entropy — its forgetting of B), energy flux (what leaks across the boundary coupling), and information (differences in B that leave registered marks in A). The cut doesn't create physics from nothing — it makes global structure locally legible. And the framework's central honesty: nothing inside the theory selects the cut itself, and nothing gives the arrow of time a direction. Those two residues — the chosen cut and the imposed arrow — are the "two turtles" the whole construction stands on.

How to read the claims

Every claim in the spec carries a status tag. This is the framework's load-bearing honesty — filtering by tag is the fastest way to see what is established versus what is hoped:

EST — established result in the literature; not original here.

FIELDS — due to Chris Fields and collaborators; assembled, not originated, by this framework.

ASM — assembly: established pieces combined, possibly original in arrangement; no new mathematics.

CONJ — conjecture: plausible, not proved, flagged open.

POST — postulate: a philosophical commitment of the ontology, adopted rather than derived.

GAP — a deliberately stated limitation: something the formalism provably cannot do.

The two turtles

The framework claims everything above bottoms out in exactly two irreducible inputs of different type — and that this pair is genuinely two, not one:

Turtle 1 — the arrow GAP

Unitary evolution is time-symmetric: SA(t) can decrease (see the Time Lab). The direction of time is an external boundary condition — a low-entropy past — imposed on the state, never derived. A constraint on the state.

Turtle 2 — the chosen cut GAP

No basis-independent, physically natural rule inside the theory selects a preferred factorization ℋA ⊗ ℋB. Every candidate selector references something exogenous, relocating the choice. A selection of a factorization — and state constraints don't induce factorizations, so the two turtles are independent.

Why there's a simulation here at all

The spec's weakest joint is emergent Lorentz invariance: a generic lattice has a lumpy, direction-dependent maximum signal speed, yet observed physics has a perfectly round light-cone. The framework's proposed mechanism — ruler cancellation — says an observer whose rods and clocks are built from the same couplings as the signals it measures cannot register the anisotropy. That is a falsifiable physics claim, so it was tested: an exact free-fermion simulation with a deliberately anisotropic substrate, an honestly-constructed endogenous observer, and pre-registered verdict rules. The result falsified the naive claim, uncovered an exact square-root law, and confirmed a corrected, infrared-only version — the full story is in the Verdict Lab.
